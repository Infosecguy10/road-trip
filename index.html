About This Trip

Virginia's Northern Neck is one of the most overlooked gems on the East Coast — a quiet, scenic peninsula wedged between the Potomac and Rappahannock rivers, stretching toward the Chesapeake Bay. Known as the "Birthplace of Presidents," it is steeped in colonial, Revolutionary War, and Civil War history. The towns are small, charming, and unhurried, with excellent seafood, waterfront inns, and colonial-era landmarks around every bend.

This July 4th weekend marks the 250th Anniversary of American Independence — making these historic grounds more meaningful than ever.

Friday, July 3 — Colonial Beach & Arrive Tappahannock

Ashburn  →  Colonial Beach  →  GW Birthplace  →  Stratford Hall  →  Tappahannock (Hampton Inn)

Pack the car and head south through King George County. This is a scenic drive through rural Virginia farmland. Aim to leave Ashburn by 8–9 a.m. to beat holiday traffic and maximize your day.

🏖️

Colonial Beach — First Stop

Colonial Beach sits right on the Potomac River and has the second-longest public beach in Virginia. It's a laid-back, charming river town with a boardwalk, kayak rentals, galleries, and excellent seafood.

  • Walk or bike the boardwalk along the Potomac
  • Rent kayaks, canoes, or stand-up paddleboards at Ferry Landing Pier
  • Browse art galleries and antique shops on Colonial Avenue
  • Watch for bald eagles — commonly spotted soaring over the Potomac here
🏛️

Historic Sites — Heading East Toward Tappahannock

  • George Washington Birthplace National Monument (1732 Popes Creek Rd, 8 mi. east of Colonial Beach) — The site where our first president was born on February 22, 1732, on his father's tobacco plantation along Popes Creek. Free to enter. Walk the grounds, tour the Memorial House, and stand at the Potomac overlook. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  • Stratford Hall (birthplace of Robert E. Lee, a few miles further east on VA-3) — One of the finest examples of colonial architecture in America, with stunning Potomac River views. Admission charged. Allow 1–1.5 hours.
💡 TipAfter Colonial Beach, head east on VA-3 — George Washington Birthplace and Stratford Hall are both on the way toward Tappahannock, so there's no backtracking required.
🍽️

Dining

  • Dockside Restaurant — Classic waterfront dining, fresh seafood, great Potomac River views. A Colonial Beach institution.
  • The Riverboat on the Potomac — Casual waterfront bar and grill, local favorite for steamed crabs and cold beer on a holiday weekend.
  • Wilkerson's Seafood — Family-owned since 1946, famous for steamed crabs and shrimp. A Northern Neck tradition.

Day 2  ·  The Big Day — 250th Anniversary of American Independence

Saturday, July 4th 🇺🇸 — Across the Northern Neck

Tappahannock (Hampton Inn)  →  Kilmarnock  →  Irvington  →  Reedville  →  Tappahannock

The big day — America's 250th birthday. Start with the Hampton Inn's free hot breakfast, then head east across the Northern Neck on VA-3 toward Kilmarnock and Irvington for a full day of history, scenery, and July 4th celebrations.

🌅

Morning — Hampton Inn, Tappahannock

Enjoy the free hot breakfast buffet at the hotel. Hit the road by 9 a.m. heading east on VA-3 — one of the most scenic drives in Virginia, passing tobacco farms, pine forests, and river glimpses all the way to Kilmarnock (~50 min).

⛪

Kilmarnock — ~1 hr from Tappahannock via VA-3

VA-3 carries you east across the heart of the Northern Neck — one of the most scenic drives in Virginia, past tobacco farms, pine forests, and glimpses of the Rappahannock. Kilmarnock is the commercial hub of the Northern Neck.

  • Historic Christ Church ⭐ — Built in 1735 by Robert "King" Carter, Virginia's wealthiest colonial planter. Widely considered the finest surviving colonial church in America. The churchyard contains Carter's tomb. Free to visit. Do not miss this.
  • Downtown Kilmarnock — 50+ shops, galleries, restaurants, and pubs in historic buildings. Great for a stroll and a browse.
  • Good Luck Cellars — Small-batch winery in the heart of the Northern Neck. Perfect for an afternoon glass.
🎡

Irvington — July 4th Parade & Afternoon

🎉 Irvington Hometown Parade ✅ Confirmed 2026 — 3 PM, July 4

King Carter Drive, Irvington, VA · Free · No tickets needed
Decorated golf carts, antique cars, the Kilmarnock & District Pipe Band, bicycles, and community floats. Car show follows at the Town Commons. A quintessential small-town American Fourth of July.

  • Steamboat Era Museum — Fascinating small museum documenting the golden age of Chesapeake Bay steamboats that served these river towns. Located right in Irvington.
  • Dog & Oyster Vineyard — A beloved micro-vineyard just outside Irvington pairing local wine with fresh oysters on a beautiful rural property. A perfect afternoon stop before dinner.
  • Hope & Glory Inn area — A stroll around Irvington's picturesque waterfront and inn district is worth the time on the holiday.
🍽️

Dinner — Before the Fireworks

  • Filibuster at the Kilmarnock Inn — Great July 4th atmosphere in town. Excellent cocktails and creative menu. Perfect before the drive to Reedville for fireworks.
  • 1710 Tavern, Tappahannock — If you'd rather eat back at base after the fireworks, this is the top dinner spot in town.
🎆

Evening — Reedville Fireworks over the Water

🎇 Reedville July 4th Fireworks — ~9:15 PM

Reedville Fishermen's Museum area, 504 Main St, Reedville, VA · Free
Reedville's working waterfront and active fishing fleet make for a spectacular backdrop — fireworks over the Chesapeake Bay at ~9:15 PM. One of the most memorable fireworks settings in Virginia. Drive north from Irvington ~20 min via VA-360. Return to Hampton Inn Tappahannock (~45 min) after.

🎆 Evening Plan After dinner in Kilmarnock or Irvington, drive north to Reedville (~20 min from Irvington via VA-360) for fireworks over the water at ~9:15 PM. The drive back to Hampton Inn Tappahannock is about 45 minutes — an easy, relaxed end to America's 250th birthday.
Day 3  ·  Oysters, Waterways & the Middle Peninsula

Sunday, July 5 — White Stone, Topping & Urbanna

Tappahannock (Hampton Inn)  →  White Stone  →  Topping/Merroir  →  Urbanna  →  Tappahannock (fireworks)

A leisurely day sweeping east to the tip of the Northern Neck, crossing the Rappahannock, and looping back along the Middle Peninsula. Today is about water, oysters, and the slower rhythms of Chesapeake Bay life — with fireworks waiting back at home base tonight.

🌅

Morning — Hampton Inn Breakfast & Drive to White Stone

Start with the free hot breakfast, then head east on VA-3 (~55 min) directly to White Stone. Today skips Irvington and Kilmarnock — you covered those thoroughly on Day 2 — and focuses on fresh ground: the river crossing, oysters, and Urbanna.

  • Free hot breakfast at Hampton Inn before hitting the road
  • Scenic drive east on VA-3 along the Rappahannock to White Stone (~55 min)
  • White Stone waterfront — Walk along the Rappahannock with sweeping views across to the Middle Peninsula and the Norris Bridge
🍽️

Lunch — Adrift, White Stone

🌟 Don't Miss: Adrift Restaurant

572 Rappahannock Dr., White Stone, VA · Reserve well in advance

Chef Devin Rose's Adrift is the Northern Neck's most celebrated restaurant — refined coastal cuisine in a relaxed waterfront setting right on the Rappahannock. Have lunch here before crossing the Norris Bridge, so you stay on the right side of the river without backtracking.

🦪

Afternoon — Merroir, Topping

🌟 Don't Miss: Merroir Tasting Room

784 Locklies Creek Rd, Topping, VA  ·  rroysters.com

Merroir is the waterfront tasting room of the Rappahannock Oyster Company, right on the south bank of the Rappahannock with views directly over their oyster beds. Everything is served raw or grilled outdoors, small-plate style.

  • Rappahannock River oysters — sweet and buttery; the original variety from these beds
  • Stingray oysters — slightly saltier, grown further downriver
  • Olde Salt oysters — boldly briny, from Chesapeake Bay-exposed beds
  • Menus are seasonal and ever-changing; paired with craft beers and carefully selected wines
  • Reservations strongly recommended for summer weekends
🚣

Urbanna & the Middle Peninsula

Cross the Rappahannock River to the Middle Peninsula — quieter and even less visited than the Northern Neck.

  • Urbanna (note the spelling) — A charming working harbor with historic waterfront buildings. Home of the famous Urbanna Oyster Festival each November.
  • Walk the waterfront and downtown — a great spot to reflect on the weekend's journey before heading back to the inn.
🍽️

Dinner Back at Base

  • 1710 Tavern or Lowery's, Tappahannock — Light dinner back at base before heading to Essex High School for the 6 PM softball and 9 PM fireworks.
💡 Timing TipAim to leave Urbanna by 4:30 PM — it's about 45 min back to Tappahannock via VA-33 west to US-17 north, giving you time for a quick dinner before the 6 PM softball at Essex High School and 9 PM fireworks.

Day 4  ·  ~90 miles · ~2 hrs back to Ashburn (with stops)

Monday, July 6 — Homeward Bound

Tappahannock (check-out 11 AM)  →  Port Royal  →  Fredericksburg (lunch)  →  Ashburn

Check-out is at 11 a.m. — enjoy the free breakfast one last time, then a scenic drive home with two worthwhile stops along the way. July 4th weekend traffic is mostly heading the other direction, so the return to Ashburn is relaxed.

🌅

Before You Leave — Tappahannock

  • Final hot breakfast at the Hampton Inn
  • Quick stroll along the Rappahannock waterfront or Prince Street before checkout
🏛️

Stop 1 — Port Royal, VA (~15 min north on US-17)

One of Virginia's oldest river ports, established in 1652 as a tobacco shipping hub on the Rappahannock. But Port Royal's most remarkable claim to history: John Wilkes Booth was cornered and killed here on April 26, 1865 — just 12 days after assassinating President Lincoln — at the Garrett Farm, 2 miles south of town.

  • Booth's capture site marker — Roadside historical marker on US-301 (James Madison Pkwy), approximately 3 miles south of Port Royal on the left heading north. Easy pull-off — no address, just watch for it on US-301.
  • Port Royal historic district — Main St, Port Royal, VA 22535 — A handful of well-preserved 18th-century buildings along the Rappahannock; worth a 15-minute walk.
  • A fitting final chapter to a weekend spent walking America's founding history
🍽️

Stop 2 — Fredericksburg, VA (~30 min north of Port Royal)

Fredericksburg's 40-block National Historic District is one of the most intact colonial and Civil War streetscapes in America — 350+ original 18th and 19th century buildings, excellent restaurants, and a compact, walkable downtown. A natural lunch stop right on the route home.

  • Downtown Historic District — Caroline St, Fredericksburg, VA 22401 — Stroll Caroline Street past colonial taverns, independent shops, and Civil War-era buildings. Great lunch restaurants throughout the 900–1100 blocks.
  • George Washington's Ferry Farm — 268 George Washington Way, Fredericksburg, VA 22405 — Washington's boyhood home, just across the Rappahannock from downtown (Stafford County side). Guided house tours available; grounds walkable on their own. Note: some GPS systems use the old address — this new entrance is at the stoplight at Ferry Rd & Kings Hwy.
  • Mary Washington House — 1200 Charles St, Fredericksburg, VA 22401 — Washington purchased this home for his mother in 1772; she lived here until her death in 1789.
  • Fredericksburg & Spotsylvania National Military Park — 1013 Lafayette Blvd, Fredericksburg, VA 22401 — Four major Civil War battlefields preserved together; visitor center is free.
💡 Timing Arrive Fredericksburg around 12:30 PM, enjoy a 1.5-hour lunch in the historic district, then it's an easy 45-minute drive home to Ashburn — arriving by 3 PM.
🚗

Route Home from Fredericksburg

Take I-95 N to I-66 W (or US-29 N) from Fredericksburg to Ashburn — about 45 miles, roughly 45–60 minutes depending on traffic. A relaxed finish to a memorable long weekend.

🍷 Optional Detour Ingleside Winery (5872 Leedstown Rd, Oak Grove, VA) is one of Virginia's oldest wineries, set in the Northern Neck countryside. Note: Oak Grove is back toward the Colonial Beach area — not on the direct route to Fredericksburg. If you'd like to visit, add it as an early morning detour before Port Royal, or grab a bottle from a local shop in Tappahannock instead.

🎒 What to Pack

  • Sunscreen and wide-brim hats — the Northern Neck sun off the water is intense in July
  • Bug spray — marsh mosquitoes emerge in the evenings
  • Casual, comfortable clothes — smart casual is the dressiest you'll need anywhere on this trip
  • Binoculars — excellent bald eagle, osprey, and great blue heron sightings along the rivers
  • A small cooler — useful for any fresh seafood or wine you want to bring home
  • Cash — some smaller establishments prefer it

🚗 Getting Around & Traffic

You will need your car for the full route — there is no public transit between these towns. Most destinations are walkable once you arrive. Plan on ~50 minutes each way between Tappahannock and Kilmarnock/Irvington on VA-3 — a genuinely scenic drive through the heart of the Northern Neck.

Traffic leaving the DC area on Friday July 3rd can be heavy after noon. Depart Ashburn by 8–9 a.m. to beat the rush. Return traffic on Monday will be lighter as you're heading west while most beach traffic heads north.

🏛️ Historical Context

The Northern Neck produced an astonishing concentration of American founding figures: George Washington (Pope's Creek), James Madison (Belle Grove, nearby), James Monroe (Monroe Hall), and Robert E. Lee (Stratford Hall). Walking these grounds during the 250th anniversary of American independence adds a layer of meaning that is genuinely rare. Take your time at the historic sites — they are the heart of this trip.
