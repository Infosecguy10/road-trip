About This Trip

Virginia's Northern Neck is one of the most overlooked gems on the East Coast — a quiet, scenic peninsula wedged between the Potomac and Rappahannock rivers, stretching toward the Chesapeake Bay. Known as the "Birthplace of Presidents," it is steeped in colonial, Revolutionary War, and Civil War history. The towns are small, charming, and unhurried, with excellent seafood, waterfront inns, and colonial-era landmarks around every bend.

This July 4th weekend marks the 250th Anniversary of American Independence — making these historic grounds more meaningful than ever.

Day 1  ·  ~130 miles · ~2.5 hrs from Ashburn via US-301 S

Friday, July 3 — Colonial Beach & Arrive Tappahannock

Ashburn  →  [Popes Creek / Stratford Hall]  →  Colonial Beach  →  Tappahannock (Hampton Inn)

Pack the car and head south through King George County. This is a scenic drive through rural Virginia farmland. Aim to leave Ashburn by 8–9 a.m. to beat holiday traffic and maximize your day.

🏛️

Historic Sites — En Route

  • George Washington Birthplace National Monument (1732 Popes Creek Rd, 8 mi. from Colonial Beach) — The site where our first president was born on February 22, 1732, on his father's tobacco plantation along Popes Creek. Free to enter. Walk the grounds, tour the Memorial House, and stand at the Potomac overlook. Allow 1.5–2 hours.
  • Stratford Hall (birthplace of Robert E. Lee, just a few miles further east) — One of the finest examples of colonial architecture in America, with stunning Potomac River views. Admission charged. Allow 1–1.5 hours.
💡 TipVisit Popes Creek and Stratford Hall first thing in the morning — both are east of Colonial Beach on VA-3/VA-204, so you'll pass them on your way in. Save the beach for the afternoon.
🏖️

Colonial Beach

Colonial Beach sits right on the Potomac River and has the second-longest public beach in Virginia. It's a laid-back, charming river town with a boardwalk, kayak rentals, galleries, and excellent seafood.

  • Walk or bike the boardwalk along the Potomac
  • Rent kayaks, canoes, or stand-up paddleboards at Ferry Landing Pier
  • Browse art galleries and antique shops on Colonial Avenue
  • Watch for bald eagles — commonly spotted soaring over the Potomac here
🍽️

Dining

  • Dockside Restaurant — Classic waterfront dining, fresh seafood, great Potomac River views. A Colonial Beach institution.
  • The Riverboat on the Potomac — Casual waterfront bar and grill, local favorite for steamed crabs and cold beer on a holiday weekend.
  • Wilkerson's Seafood — Family-owned since 1946, famous for steamed crabs and shrimp. A Northern Neck tradition.

Day 2  ·  The Big Day — 250th Anniversary of American Independence

Saturday, July 4th 🇺🇸 — Across the Northern Neck

Tappahannock (Hampton Inn)  →  Kilmarnock  →  Irvington  →  Reedville  →  Tappahannock

The big day — America's 250th birthday. Start with the Hampton Inn's free hot breakfast, then head east across the Northern Neck on VA-3 toward Kilmarnock and Irvington for a full day of history, scenery, and July 4th celebrations.

🌅

Morning — Hampton Inn, Tappahannock

Enjoy the free hot breakfast buffet at the hotel. Hit the road by 9 a.m. heading east on VA-3 — one of the most scenic drives in Virginia, passing tobacco farms, pine forests, and river glimpses all the way to Kilmarnock (~50 min).

🏛️

Tappahannock Historic District — On Your Doorstep

A gem of a colonial river town on the Rappahannock, with a beautifully preserved historic district. It was occupied by the British during the War of 1812, and walking its streets feels like stepping back centuries.

  • Essex County Museum — Free admission. Features the Debtors' Jail (1700s), colonial-era artifacts, and exhibits on Rappahannock River steamboats and the War of 1812.
  • Historic District Walk — Stroll along Prince Street and Water Lane past colonial warehouses, the old courthouse, and historic homes. Pick up a walking map at the museum.
  • Rappahannock River Waterfront — Beautiful views, fishing pier, and a powerful sense of timelessness.
🍽️

Lunch in Tappahannock

  • 1710 Tavern — Modern interpretations of colonial-era food with locally sourced ingredients. A standout for the region.
  • Lowery's Restaurant — A beloved local institution on the Rappahannock, known for fresh seafood and regional classics.
⛪

Kilmarnock — ~1 hr from Tappahannock via VA-3

VA-3 carries you east across the heart of the Northern Neck — one of the most scenic drives in Virginia, past tobacco farms, pine forests, and glimpses of the Rappahannock. Kilmarnock is the commercial hub of the Northern Neck.

  • Historic Christ Church ⭐ — Built in 1735 by Robert "King" Carter, Virginia's wealthiest colonial planter. Widely considered the finest surviving colonial church in America. The churchyard contains Carter's tomb. Free to visit. Do not miss this.
  • Downtown Kilmarnock — 50+ shops, galleries, restaurants, and pubs in historic buildings. Great for a stroll and a browse.
  • Good Luck Cellars — Small-batch winery in the heart of the Northern Neck. Perfect for an afternoon glass.
  • Dog & Oyster Vineyard — A beloved micro-vineyard just outside Irvington pairing local wine with fresh oysters on a beautiful rural property.
🚗

Evening — Wind Vineyards & Fireworks (10 min from hotel)

Head to Wind Vineyards by 4 PM for the July 4th celebration. After the fireworks, it's a short 10-minute drive back to the Hampton Inn — a perfect end to America's 250th birthday.

🎆

July 4th Fireworks & Celebrations

🎇 Wind Vineyards July 4th Celebration — Tappahannock

854 Dunbrooke Rd, Tappahannock · 4 PM start · Tickets required
The big event right at your home base. Afternoon of live music, vendors & food trucks, Field of Fun (slides & games), hay rides, Grape Crusher rides, and a Walking Tall Monster Truck freestyle run. Evening closes with a fireworks display over the vineyard. Buy tickets in advance — this sells out.
→ Get tickets at RunSignUp.com

  • Irvington Hometown Parade ✅ Confirmed 2026 — 3 PM, July 4 on King Carter Drive. Decorated golf carts, antique cars, the Kilmarnock & District Pipe Band, bicycles, and community floats. Car show follows at the Town Commons. A quintessential small-town American parade.
  • Reedville July 4th Celebration — Reedville Fishermen's Museum area, ~30 min north of Tappahannock via VA-360. Fireworks over the water. A spectacular Chesapeake Bay setting — worth the drive if you skip Wind Vineyards.
💡 Plan Your Evening You have two great options — pick one:

Option A (stay close to base): Catch the Irvington parade at 3 PM → drive back to Wind Vineyards by 4 PM for the full July 4th festival + fireworks (~10 min from hotel).

Option B (scenic fireworks): Enjoy Irvington fully in the afternoon → drive to Reedville for fireworks over the water at ~9:15 PM (then 45 min back to Hampton Inn).
🍽️

Dinner

  • Filibuster at the Kilmarnock Inn — Great July 4th atmosphere in town. Excellent cocktails and creative menu. Perfect before the drive to Reedville for fireworks.
  • 1710 Tavern, Tappahannock — If you'd rather eat back at base after the fireworks, this is the top dinner spot in town.
Day 3  ·  Oysters, Waterways & the Middle Peninsula

Sunday, July 5 — White Stone, Topping & Urbana

Tappahannock (Hampton Inn)  →  Irvington  →  White Stone  →  Topping/Merroir  →  Urbana  →  Tappahannock

A leisurely day exploring the lower Northern Neck and crossing to the Middle Peninsula. Today is about water, oysters, and the slower rhythms of Chesapeake Bay life. Head east on VA-3 again after the Hampton Inn breakfast (~50 min to Irvington).

🌅

Morning — Hampton Inn Breakfast & Drive East

Start with the free hot breakfast at the hotel, then take the scenic VA-3 east to Irvington. Stop in Kilmarnock if you want a coffee at The Local before continuing to the tip of the Northern Neck.

  • Free hot breakfast at Hampton Inn before hitting the road
  • The Local in Kilmarnock — excellent locally roasted coffee and fresh pastries en route
  • Arrive in Irvington by mid-morning for a relaxed start to the day
🏘️

Irvington & White Stone

  • Steamboat Era Museum (Irvington) — Tells the story of the Chesapeake Bay steamboat trade that once connected these communities to Baltimore and Washington. Small but fascinating.
  • Local shops and galleries in Irvington village — worth a leisurely browse on a Sunday morning.
  • White Stone waterfront — Lovely walk along the Rappahannock with views across to the Middle Peninsula.
🦪

The Meal of the Trip — Merroir, Topping

🌟 Don't Miss: Merroir Tasting Room

784 Locklies Creek Rd, Topping, VA  ·  rroysters.com

Merroir is the waterfront tasting room of the Rappahannock Oyster Company, right on the south bank of the Rappahannock with views directly over their oyster beds. Everything is served raw or grilled outdoors, small-plate style.

  • Rappahannock River oysters — sweet and buttery; the original variety from these beds
  • Stingray oysters — slightly saltier, grown further downriver
  • Olde Salt oysters — boldly briny, from Chesapeake Bay-exposed beds
  • Menus are seasonal and ever-changing; paired with craft beers and carefully selected wines
  • Reservations strongly recommended for summer weekends
🚣

Urbana & the Middle Peninsula

Cross the Rappahannock River to the Middle Peninsula — quieter and even less visited than the Northern Neck.

  • Urbanna (note the spelling) — A charming working harbor with historic waterfront buildings. Home of the famous Urbanna Oyster Festival each November.
  • Walk the waterfront and downtown — a great spot to reflect on the weekend's journey before heading back to the inn.
🍽️

Dinner

  • Adrift (572 Rappahannock Dr., White Stone) — Run by chef Devin Rose, Adrift is the Northern Neck's most celebrated restaurant. Refined coastal cuisine in a relaxed waterfront setting. Reserve well in advance.
  • 1710 Tavern, Tappahannock — A great fallback for dinner back at base if Adrift isn't available.

Day 4  ·  ~90 miles · ~1.5 hrs back to Ashburn

Monday, July 6 — Homeward Bound

Tappahannock (Hampton Inn, check-out 11 AM)  →  Ashburn

Check-out is at 11 a.m. — enjoy the free breakfast one last time, then an easy drive home. July 4th weekend traffic is mostly heading the other direction, so the return to Ashburn is stress-free.

🌅

Before You Leave

  • Final hot breakfast at the Hampton Inn
  • Walk along the Rappahannock waterfront in Tappahannock — a peaceful morning stroll before the drive home
  • Browse the boutiques and antique shops on Prince Street if shops are open before 11 a.m.
🚗

Route Home

From Tappahannock, take US-17 N or US-301 N back toward Fredericksburg, then connect to I-95 N or US-17 N to Ashburn. A straightforward ~90-mile drive, about 1.5 hours with no traffic.

🍷 Bonus StopIngleside Winery (Oak Grove, just north of Colonial Beach on VA-638) is one of Virginia's oldest and most respected wineries, set in beautiful Northern Neck countryside. A great bottle to bring home as a trip memento.

🎒 What to Pack

  • Sunscreen and wide-brim hats — the Northern Neck sun off the water is intense in July
  • Bug spray — marsh mosquitoes emerge in the evenings
  • Casual, comfortable clothes — smart casual is the dressiest you'll need anywhere on this trip
  • Binoculars — excellent bald eagle, osprey, and great blue heron sightings along the rivers
  • A small cooler — useful for any fresh seafood or wine you want to bring home
  • Cash — some smaller establishments prefer it

🚗 Getting Around & Traffic

You will need your car for the full route — there is no public transit between these towns. Most destinations are walkable once you arrive. Plan on ~50 minutes each way between Tappahannock and Kilmarnock/Irvington on VA-3 — a genuinely scenic drive through the heart of the Northern Neck.

Traffic leaving the DC area on Friday July 3rd can be heavy after noon. Depart Ashburn by 8–9 a.m. to beat the rush. Return traffic on Monday will be lighter as you're heading west while most beach traffic heads north.

🏛️ Historical Context

The Northern Neck produced an astonishing concentration of American founding figures: George Washington (Pope's Creek), James Madison (Belle Grove, nearby), James Monroe (Monroe Hall), and Robert E. Lee (Stratford Hall). Walking these grounds during the 250th anniversary of American independence adds a layer of meaning that is genuinely rare. Take your time at the historic sites — they are the heart of this trip.
